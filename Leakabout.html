What is a Leak Test?

Verification of airtightness in components and systems across production lines.

Leak tests allow for the verification of airtightness in components and systems. They are used in production lines to detect defects in valves, seals, assemblies, heat exchangers, and critical components — preventing failures, ensuring compliance with quality standards, reducing waste, and guaranteeing safety in pressurised products.

Method 01
Pressure Drop
Standard method for detecting leaks by measuring pressure variation. Ideal for automated lines and high-volume quality control.
Method 02
Vacuum
Evaluates hermeticity through controlled vacuum extraction. Used in electronic and medical applications.
Method 03
Helium Tracer Gas
High-precision detection of microleaks in critical components. Standard in medical, aerospace, and advanced automotive.
Method 04
Water Sink
Basic visual inspection by immersion or soapy solution for fundamental, low-risk leak detection.

Frequently Asked

Which method is most accurate? +
Helium tracer gas provides the highest accuracy for detecting microleaks in critical applications such as medical devices and aerospace components.
What industries use leak testing? +
Automotive, medical, electronics, aerospace, food packaging, and general industrial manufacturing all rely on leak testing for quality assurance and regulatory compliance.
Can leak testing be automated? +
Yes. Modern equipment supports complete integration into production lines with real-time validation, reduced cycle times, and compliance with industrial standards.
